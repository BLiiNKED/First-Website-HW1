    Why Drums Are Hard To Master

    by Christian

    Drums vs. Guitar

    People often ask if it's harder to learn drums or guitar. Drums tend to be harder for most because you'll need to master a three-way coordination just to get started. With guitars, you can learn basic chords quickly and usually get a nice sound without any knowledge which makes it less reliant on rhythm. However, drums rely more on keeping a reliable time. Apart from keeping the rhythm of the entire band, playing drums is also more physically demanding; requiring you to use both arms and legs to play the instrument.

    Advantages of Learning Drums

    There are many upsides to learning the drums, especially if they are your first instrument. It helps build a good sense of rhythm for learning other instruments. This will help you build a inner-sense of timing. Also, once you master one beat, you can learn a ton of others easily. It's hard to work your hands and feet seperately, but once you get past that roadblock then the rest is way easier. If interested, drummers are also in high demand. Being the rhythm of the band, you have no room for mistake like guitars since they're more obvious, hence the standard for drummers is higher. This means there are more gigs as a drummer than you might initially assume.
